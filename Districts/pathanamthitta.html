Pathanamthitta

The state of Kerala is divided into 14 districts. Each district, with its plethora of destinations and rich culture, attracts tourists from all around the world. We've made a list of destinations from each district, which we know you'll enjoy.

Vagamon

Vagamon is mainly a hill station with an overtone of green. Listed by the National Geographic Traveler their directory of the "50 most attractive places to visit in India", Vagamon has lush green hills, breathtaking ravines, meandering rivulets, small waterfalls and pine forests. Tourists can also try paragliding and other activities here.

Fort Kochi

Fort Kochi, is a locality 16 km from Cochin city and takes its name from Fort Manuel of Cochin, the first European fort on Indian soil. This is part of a handful of water-bound islands and islets toward the south-west of mainland Kochi, and collectively known as Old Cochin or West Kochi. Its colourful streets, churches, synagogues, hotels and beaches make it a famous tourist destination. The chinese fish nets here are a must see.

Hill Palace

Hill Palace museum is the largest archeological museum in the state and was the imperial administrative office and official residence of the Cochin Maharaja. Built in 1865, the palace complex consists of 49 buildings in the traditional architectural style, spreading across 54 acres. The complex has an archaeological museum, a heritage museum, a deer park, a pre-historic park and a children’s park.
