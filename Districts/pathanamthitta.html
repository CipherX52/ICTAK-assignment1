Pathanamthitta

Sabarimala, one of the most devout temples of Lord Ayyappa, is located at Pathanamthitta, which is also famous for its handmade metal-alloy mirrors, "Aranmula kannadi" and scenic forests.

Gavi

Gavi is a beautiful village inside the Ranni reserve forest.It is a part of the Periyar Tiger Reserve. Forest tent camping is available from November through March. Gavi's forest area, there are two gopher trees and they are believed to be the only two gopher trees in India.Gavi's evergreen forests are abundant with exotic flora and fauna. Apparently gopher trees in India are seen only at Gavi.

Thrivenisangamam

Situated on the way to Sabarimala, the Thriveni Sanganam as the name suggests is the point where the Holy Pamba River meets with the Manimala River in its north and with the Achankovil River in the south.Devotees believe that it was near this holy river that King Rajasekara gained a vision of Lord Ayappa.

Perunthenaruvi

Located on the Western Ghats of the Sahyadri Range, Perunthenaruvi is famous for the waterfalls there. The waterfalls are known for their wide area, rather than their height. The stream later unites with the Pamba River. It is beautiful and dangerous at the same time.
