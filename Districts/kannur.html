Kannur

The state of Kerala is divided into 14 districts. Each district, with its plethora of destinations and rich culture, attracts tourists from all around the world. We've made a list of destinations from each district, which we know you'll enjoy.

Aaralam Farm

The Aralam Farm and Wildlife Sanctuary is evergreen and deciduous forest area spanning 55 sq. kms on the slope of the Western Ghats. The Aralam Sanctuary is home to a wide array of flora and fauna, the only wildlife sanctuary in Kannur district, and the best spot for eco-tourists.

Muzhappilangad Beach

Muzhappilangad Drive-in Beach is a beach on the Malabar Coast, parallel to National Highway 66 and is the longest drive-in beach in Asia. The beach is bordered by black rocks, which also protects it from the stronger ocean currents. These rocks provide habitat for Blue Mussel, a local delicacy. It is also a bird watching hotspot.

Paithalmala

Paithalmala is a hill station located near Pottenplave Village. At a height of 1372m above sea level, this is the highest geographic peak in Kannur. Nestled in the Kerala-Karnataka border near the Kodagu forests, it lies in the Western Ghats. Trekking is the major activity here. There is an observatory atop the hill, which is a 45 min trek from Paithal Valley.
