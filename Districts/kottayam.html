Kottayam

The state of Kerala is divided into 14 districts. Each district, with its plethora of destinations and rich culture, attracts tourists from all around the world. We've made a list of destinations from each district, which we know you'll enjoy.

Vagamon

Vagamon is a hill station with an overtone of green. Listed by the National Geographic Traveler their directory of the "50 most attractive places to visit in India", Vagamon has lush green hills, breathtaking ravines, meandering rivulets, small waterfalls and pine forests. Tourists can also try paragliding and other activities here.

Panachikkad Temple

The Panachikkadu Temple, also known as the Dakshina Mookambika (Mookambika of the South) is one of the most prominent Saraswati temples for devotees in Kerala even though the main deity of the temple is Lord Vishnu.There are lots of interesting legends about this temple and festivals like Navrathri are celebrated with much pomp.

Kumarakom

Sprawling across 14 hectares, and sitting by the banks of the river Vembanad, Kumarakom bird sanctuary is a vibrant hub of rare and numerous avian species. It is also famous for the luxurious houseboats and dazzling backwaters.
